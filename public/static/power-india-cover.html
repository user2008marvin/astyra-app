Publication Synopsis · Strategic Overview · 2025 Edition
Power India 2047 is the flagship strategic publication of the Association of Power Producers India, examining the sector's transition from capacity expansion to systemic transformation. As India approaches 300 GW of renewable capacity by 2030 and aligns its energy architecture with the Viksit Bharat 2047 vision, this publication provides the definitive reference for policy makers, investors, and industry leaders navigating the next phase of India's power evolution.
Chapter 01
System Transformation
India's power sector has moved beyond capacity expansion into a complex phase of reliability, flexibility and efficiency optimisation within a diversified energy mix. Peak demand at record levels; renewables growing at 20%+ annually.
Chapter 02
Renewable Acceleration & Integration
Solar and wind capacity additions at record pace. Green Energy Corridors, hybrid RTC tenders, and ALMM domestic manufacturing framework targeting 100 GW+. Integration and curtailment challenges addressed.
Chapter 03
Grid Reliability, Flexibility & Storage
BESS deployment with VGF support, pumped hydro as long-duration storage, coal flexibilisation below technical minimum, and ancillary services market development for frequency regulation.
Chapter 04
Transmission & Infrastructure
Inter-state network acceleration, high-voltage infrastructure connecting generation hubs to demand centres, digital grid monitoring and management technologies, and investment opportunities in transmission corridors.
Chapter 05 – 06
DISCOM Reform & Policy Evolution
RDSS implementation, smart prepaid metering, AT&C loss reduction, ToD tariffs, DBT subsidy mechanisms. Alongside regulatory reform, competitive bidding, open access expansion, and private sector participation.
Chapter 07 – 08
Digitalisation & Emerging Themes
AI-based forecasting, predictive maintenance, SCADA and ADMS smart grid systems. Climate resilience, just transition for coal regions, green hydrogen ecosystems and electric mobility infrastructure.
💡
Chapter 09 — Investment Landscape: India is positioned as one of the world's largest and most attractive power sector investment destinations. Opportunities span renewable energy, hybrid systems, battery storage, transmission modernisation, smart metering, and industrial decarbonisation — with strong policy direction, market depth, and institutional capacity underpinning investor confidence.
