Special Edition · Volume XII · New Delhi

Power India 2047 is the flagship strategic publication of the Association of Power Producers India (APP), examining the sector's transition from capacity expansion to systemic transformation. As India approaches 300 GW of renewable capacity by 2030 and aligns its energy architecture with the Viksit Bharat 2047 national vision, this publication provides the definitive strategic reference for policy makers, investors, regulators, and industry leaders navigating the next critical phase of India's power evolution. The publication presents India's power sector not only as a story of scale, but as a model of system transformation at national level.

Chapter 01
From Capacity Expansion to System Transformation
India's power sector has moved beyond capacity expansion into a complex phase of reliability, flexibility and efficiency optimisation within a diversified energy mix. Peak demand is at record levels; renewables growing at 20%+ annually. The publication frames India as a global model of national-scale energy transformation.
Chapter 02
Renewable Energy Acceleration & Integration
  • Solar and wind capacity additions at record pace — 20%+ annual growth
  • Green Energy Corridors for large-scale power evacuation
  • Hybrid and round-the-clock (RTC) renewable tenders
  • ALMM domestic manufacturing framework targeting 100 GW+
  • Grid congestion, curtailment risks and integration strategies
Chapter 03
Grid Reliability, Flexibility & Storage
  • Peak demand management and dispatchable capacity requirements
  • BESS deployment underpinned by Viability Gap Funding (VGF)
  • Pumped storage projects as long-duration storage solutions
  • Coal plant flexibilisation below technical minimum thresholds
  • Ancillary services markets for frequency regulation
Chapter 04
Transmission Expansion & Infrastructure Modernisation
  • Inter-state transmission network acceleration
  • High-voltage infrastructure linking generation hubs to demand centres
  • Digital monitoring and grid management technologies
  • Investment opportunities across transmission corridors
  • Structural alignment of expansion pace with generation growth
Chapter 05
Distribution Sector Reform — DISCOM Transformation
  • Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme (RDSS) implementation
  • Smart prepaid metering rollout at national scale
  • AT&C loss reduction and billing efficiency improvements
  • Financial restructuring for long-term DISCOM viability
  • Time-of-Day tariffs and DBT subsidy mechanisms
Chapter 06
Policy, Regulation & Market Evolution
  • Electricity Act amendments and structural policy reforms
  • Competitive bidding frameworks across generation and storage
  • Open access market and corporate PPA expansion
  • Power exchanges and market-based dispatch mechanisms
  • Private sector participation in nuclear energy
Chapters 07 – 08
Digitalisation, Smart Systems & Emerging Themes
  • AI-based forecasting for demand and renewable generation
  • Predictive maintenance, SCADA and ADMS smart grid deployment
  • Climate resilience and grid adaptation to extreme weather
  • Just transition strategies for coal-dependent regions
  • Green hydrogen and electric mobility ecosystem development
⚡
Chapter 09 — Investment Landscape & Opportunities
India represents one of the world's largest and most attractive power sector investment markets, with opportunities spanning renewable energy and hybrid systems, battery storage, transmission modernisation, smart metering, and industrial decarbonisation. Key verticals include corporate PPA procurement and green hydrogen infrastructure. With strong policy direction, deep market capacity, and robust institutional frameworks, India is firmly positioned as a leading global destination for long-term energy capital — offering both scale and structural investment certainty across the full value chain of the power sector.
