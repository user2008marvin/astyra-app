Section 01
What is Astyra?

Overview

Astyra is an AI-powered makeup look engine built around a single idea: your makeup should be chosen based on where you are going, not on a random filter or generic recommendation.

Instead of asking you to try on virtual lipstick colours or apply cartoon-style filters, Astyra asks two precise questions — what occasion are you dressing for? and what is your skin tone? — and then generates three complete, expert-level makeup looks specifically calibrated for that combination.

Each look includes exact colour names and hex codes for lips, eyes, and blush; a step-by-step application sequence; expert application tips; and direct links to real products you can buy.

The Core Concept: Occasion Intelligence

Most makeup apps show you products. Astyra shows you strategy. There is a meaningful difference between the makeup that works for a corporate boardroom presentation and the makeup that works for a festival. Between a wedding guest look and a first date look. Between a graduation ceremony and a night out.

Astyra has a dedicated look library for all 12 occasion types, each mapped across 6 skin tones and 3 look styles (Natural, Glam, and Trendy). The result is up to 216 distinct look combinations — all without any AI image generation or filter technology.

✦
Design Philosophy
Astyra deliberately avoids AR face filters and virtual try-on. The app competes on occasion-based intelligence — knowing what works for your event context — not on image processing. This makes it faster, more private, and more useful.

Section 02
Getting Started — The 4-Step Flow

Astyra takes approximately 60 seconds from opening the app to seeing your personalised look report. The flow is intentionally simple and requires no account, no sign-up, and no camera access beyond selecting a photo from your library.

1
Upload Your Bare Face Photo
Tap the upload zone (📷) and select a photo. For best results, use a photo with no makeup, good natural lighting, and your face looking straight at the camera. You can also drag and drop a photo directly onto the upload zone on desktop. Your photo never leaves your device — it is processed entirely within your browser.
2
Select Your Occasion
After uploading, a grid of 12 occasion cards appears below your photo preview. Tap the occasion that matches your event. Each card shows an emoji, the occasion name, and a brief description. Three occasions (Bridal, Black Tie Gala, Festival) are marked with a 👑 crown and require a Pro subscription.
3
Select Your Dermal Base Tone
Six skin tone circles appear (Fair, Light, Medium, Tan, Deep, Rich). Tap the one closest to your natural skin tone. Astyra uses this to adjust which colours within each look palette are recommended as your primary shade — ensuring the colours it suggests actually complement your complexion.
4
Initiate Look Generation
Once an occasion and skin tone are selected, an "ANALYSIS MATRIX READY" card appears with a summary of your choices and the "✦ INITIATE LOOK GENERATION" button. Tap it. A loading screen runs for approximately 3 seconds showing the 4 analysis steps in real time, then your Look Report appears.
✓
Best Photo Tips
Do: Use natural daylight, face forward, no makeup, neutral background.
Avoid: Strong filters, heavy shadows on one side, sunglasses, or photos taken at a steep angle.
The photo is only used for the preview — Astyra's look engine runs on your stated skin tone, not image analysis.
⚠
Free Scan Limit
Free users receive 3 scans per month. The scan counter is shown in the top navigation bar. When your scans reach zero, the Generate button prompts you to upgrade to Pro. Pro users get unlimited scans.

How the Look Library Works

Every occasion has three distinct look profiles: Natural/Soft, Glam/Bold, and Trendy/Editorial. Each profile contains:

  • 3 lip colour options (primary recommended based on your skin tone)
  • 3 eye colour options
  • 2 blush colour options
  • A 5-step application sequence specific to that look
  • A Astyra expert tip tailored to the occasion context
  • A skin-tone-specific note

How Calibration Works

Every look palette contains 3 lip options and 3 eye options arranged from lighter to deeper. When you select a skin tone, the system moves a "preferred index" pointer to select the best starting shade:

The calibration selects a primary recommendation — but all three shades in each palette row are shown on screen. You can tap any swatch to see its name and hex code. The primary is simply the first suggested starting point based on your tone.

Changing Your Skin Tone

If you tap the wrong skin tone, tap a different one in the same session — the selection updates immediately and the Generate button will reflect the new tone. To change after generating looks, tap "↩ New Scan" in the top navigation bar of the results screen to restart the flow.

The Admin Dashboard is accessed at /makeup-admin. It is password-protected and intended for the app owner only. Default login credentials are admin / astyra2024 — change these immediately in the Settings tab.

⚠

Change the default credentials as soon as possible. Go to Settings → Dashboard Security to update your username and password. Sessions last 24 hours and are stored in browser localStorage.

Adding Your First Sponsorship

  1. Go to Sponsorships tab
  2. Select the occasion from the dropdown (e.g. "Wedding Guest")
  3. Enter the brand name (e.g. "Charlotte Tilbury")

Section 09
Monetisation Playbook

Astyra is built with four distinct revenue streams. This section explains how each works and what you need to do to activate it.

1
Affiliate Commissions — Passive, Always-On
Set your affiliate ID in the Affiliate tab once. Every product link in the app — across all 12 occasions and all 3 look types — automatically includes your tracking tag. When a user taps a product and buys it, you earn 4–12% commission. Average basket: £35. At 4% = £1.40/conversion. At 12% = £4.20/conversion. This requires zero ongoing work once the ID is set.
2
Brand Sponsorships — Direct, Recurring Revenue
Pitch beauty brands on guaranteed first-position product placement for their target occasion. Example pitch: "Your brand appears first for every Wedding Guest look generated by Astyra — £500/month for 12 months." A single brand at £500/mo = £6,000/year. Five brands = £30,000/year. Managed entirely through the Sponsorships tab — no code changes required.
3
Pro Subscriptions — Recurring MRR
Free users hit the 3-scan limit and convert to Pro at $4.99/mo. Target conversion: 10% of monthly active users. 1,000 MAU → 100 Pro = $499 MRR. 10,000 MAU → 1,000 Pro = $4,990 MRR. Price and scan limit are configurable in the Settings tab. The freemium gate is intentionally generous enough to show value but tight enough to drive conversion.
4
Data Partnerships — Highest Value, Scalable
Your occasion search data (available in the Analytics tab) is first-party intent data. If "Wedding Guest" is your top search, Charlotte Tilbury and NARS would pay for exclusive access to that signal. Export the CSV from the Analytics tab. Present it as an "Occasion Intent Dataset" in brand pitch decks. Industry CPM for this type of data: £15–£40. At 10,000 intent events = £150–£400 per data sale.

Section 11
Frequently Asked Questions
Is my face photo private?
Yes. Your photo is processed entirely within your browser using the FileReader API. It is never uploaded to any server, never sent to an AI service, and never stored beyond your current browser session. The "🔒 PRIVATE · LOCAL PROCESSING" badge on the upload zone confirms this. When you close the browser tab, the photo data is cleared.
Why does Astyra not actually analyse my face photo?
Astyra's look engine is deliberately built around your stated inputs (occasion + skin tone) rather than image analysis. This makes the app faster, fully private, and more predictable. The look quality comes from the curated look library — not from image processing. The photo upload creates a sense of personalisation and is used for the look preview card.
What does "Dermal Base Tone" mean?
"Dermal Base Tone" is Astyra's branded term for your skin tone. It refers to the six skin tone options (Fair, Light, Medium, Tan, Deep, Rich). The term is intentionally clinical-sounding to reinforce the idea that Astyra is a system, not a filter app.
How do I know which skin tone to select?
Hold your inner forearm next to the six swatch circles and tap the one closest to your natural skin without makeup. If you are between two tones, go with the deeper one — Astyra's colour calibration tends to be more flattering when nudged slightly warmer and deeper rather than lighter.
Can I use the same look for different occasions?
Yes, but it is not recommended. For example, the Wedding Guest Natural look ("Soft Bridal Glow") uses champagne lid and dusty rose lip, while the Job Interview Natural look ("Confident Classic") uses cream ivory lid and soft rosy nude lip. The difference matters — one is celebration makeup, the other is professional makeup. Run a new scan for each occasion for the best result.
The hex colour code does not match any product I can find. What do I do?
The hex codes represent the ideal target colour for that look. Use them to find a matching shade in any brand — search "dusty rose lipstick #C17B7B" on Google or use the Sephora/ASOS colour filter. The affiliate product cards show specific recommended products, but the hex code can be matched in any brand of your choosing.
Do the affiliate product links cost extra?
No. Affiliate links are free to use. If the app owner has set an affiliate tracking ID, clicking a product link and purchasing the product earns them a commission from the retailer — the price you pay is exactly the same as buying directly.
How does the Admin Dashboard know how many scans have been run?
All scan and click data is stored in your browser's localStorage. Because Astyra runs as a client-side app, the data is local to the device where it was generated. For multi-device analytics, a server-side analytics backend (e.g. a Cloudflare D1 database or third-party analytics service) would be required as a future enhancement.
What happens if I reset my browser data?
Clearing browser localStorage will reset your scan count to 3, remove your Pro status (if activated locally), and clear all analytics data. This is expected behaviour for a localStorage-based app. In a production deployment, user accounts and Pro status would be server-side and persistent across devices and browsers.
Can I change the Pro price?
Yes. Go to Admin Dashboard → Settings → Pro Subscription Settings → Monthly Price. Save the new price. The displayed price in the app will update. Note that the actual billing integration (Stripe or similar) would need to reflect the same price change in production.
